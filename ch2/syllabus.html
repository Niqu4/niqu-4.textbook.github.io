CHAPTER DESCRIPTION


Chapter «Oil and gas factors in energy security provision» is one of the elective chapters within the course “Energy Factor in International Relations” for speciality 5B020200 – “International Relations”.

Chapter objective - to create a common understanding of the problems of energy security provision in international relations; identify basic reasons for possible confrontations and international tension points in the field of oil and gas energy security; identify methods for a comparative analysis in this area; create a knowledge of possible threats to oil and energy and methods of counteractions; understanding of international threats connected with oil and gas ownership and development; identifying reasons for international confrontations and disputes in the oil and gas sector. Students develop a comprehensive picture of oil and gas energy security policy and obtain an understanding of the possible methods of energy security provision.

During the course, students develop the skills to understand the various dimensions, which can be used to analyze international energy security.

Tasks of course: to generate skills on the basis of acquired knowledge, which are an integral part of model of the expert (Expert on the problems of the evolution of social and political factors of cooperation and perspectives for the implementation of sustainable development of energy in the framework of national interests’ security provision). The course should provide:

  • The knowledge of the basic principles of energy policy in general, different oil and gas policies, oil and gas energy security, the role of governments and companies, impact of policy instruments on the duration of international agreements.

  • The knowledge of the differences between political and economic diversification dimensions of energy security provision and the role of oil and gas resources.

  • The knowledge of energy problems of the world community, energy based relations on a global and regional scale, common mechanisms of foreign policies of different states in the oil and gas energy sector.

  • The knowledge of perspectives of future development of oil and gas as a part of energy security provision strategies.

  • Skills of written comparative analysis.

  • Skills to determine the advisability of the research topic and comparative analysis regarding oil and gas energy security.

  • Ability to work with sources and analyze them.

  • Ability to use of scientific and specialized terminology.

  • Ability to work in complex research, governmental and political environments.

  • Ability to use statistic and analytical materials.

  • Ability to understand the diversity of oil and gas energy security concepts in various social and political situations of different states.

  • Ability to understand and analyze major oil and gas energy security problems, taking into consideration specific internal and external policies of states.

  • The awareness of the technological advances in communication and the understanding of how these advances may affect the general reception of an expert opinion.

Content of lectures for the course “Oil and gas factors in energy security provision”


Module 1 Theoretical and methodological basis of energy security


Lecture 1. Definition of energy security and its types

General understanding of energy security. The multi-faceted nature of energy security. Energy security types. Long-term energy security. Short-term energy security. Types of energy security risks. Oil security. Gas security.


Lecture 2. Identification of methods of energy security provision in view of oil and gas factor

Energy security provision policies and strategies. Methodologies used for quantitative evaluations of security of supply. Decentralized energy. Global development trends in the energy security sector.


Lecture 3. Political and legal basis of energy security provision of the oil and gas sector

Historical background of oil and gas energy security problems. Lack of international understanding and cooperation in the oil and gas energy sector. Major problems of oil and gas consumption. International treaties in the sphere of oil and gas energy security provision.


Lecture 4. Energy security and threats to its provision in contemporary international relations

International politics and threats to world energy security. Energy security dimension in foreign policy. Energy security factor in foreign policy. Political and economic influence of energy rich states. Energy resources as a political tool. Foreign policies as a threat to energy security provision.


Lecture 5. The impact of the oil and gas factor on the escalation of terrorist organizations power

Oil and gas in the hands of terrorist organizations. Escalation of terrorist threats in the light of a rising level of terrorist influence in oil and gas rich states. Possible solutions of international oil and gas threats. The ISIS threat.


Lecture 6. The position of the world powers in the oil and gas energy security provision

Policy of the EU in the oil and gas sector. Actions of the USA in the light of contemporary energy security situation in the world. The role of Russia in oil and gas security provision.


Module 2: Oil and gas factors in the foreign policy of states


Lecture 7. Oil and gas policy of the European Union

General overview of the conditions of the oil and gas supply in the European Union. Main Aspects of the oil and gas policy of EU. EU dependence on oil and gas imports.


Lecture 8. The role of Central Asia in international energy security provision

Oil and gas export policies of Central Asian states. The level of development of interstate cooperation in the oil and gas production sector of CA. Chinese energy security policy and Central Asia.


Lecture 9. Factors of international controversy in the oil and gas sector

Main aspects of international relations in the oil and gas sector. Historical background of international confrontations in the oil and gas energy sphere. Political reasons for international controversy and new threats to the world order.


Lecture 10. The impact of the oil and gas factor in the dynamics of international relations

The impact of oil and gas ownership on contemporary international relations. Oil and gas energy security as a tool for political dominance. Oil and gas energy as a factor of changing international alliances.


Lecture 11. Confrontations of East and West in the oil and gas sector as a factor of the escalation of international tension

General overview of the main energy security risks. The impact of oil and gas ownership on political and economic decision-making. General understanding of oil and gas based political and economic confrontations of West and East. Iran question.


Lecture 12. International problems connected with the oil and gas policy of the states

Major strategies of international oil and gas energy security provision policies. Political confrontations behind oil and gas security provision problems. Oil and gas resources shortage. Oil and gas price changes as a threat to global energy security and international relations.


Lecture 13. Methods and mechanisms of regulation of international oil and gas confrontations. Perspectives of international cooperation

International regulatory policies in the energy security sector. Political and legal basis for international oil and gas relations. Contemporary level of development of international cooperation in energy security provision, including oil and gas security.

The methodical recommendations for studying the discipline “Oil and gas factors in energy security provision”

The purpose of these methodological guidelines is to present the basic requirements of Research Paper (hereinafter – RP) for students studying the discipline “Oil and gas factors in energy security provision”.

Students have to deliver written work of different complexity:

Essay.This is the smallest scale, well-structured, consistent, easy-to-read written work, where the student can clearly express his/her approach, analysis and solutions to different claims and problems presented in scientific literature.

Descriptive or synoptic paper. This pattern of work is greater in volume than the essay, it presents scientifically valid opinions and their critical evaluation in a consistent way and the assessment of this type of written work usually makes up a solid part of accumulative mark.

Research paper.It is written in frequent consultation with supervisors. Its goal is to deepen the knowledge acquired during lectures and practical seminars, to develop students’ critical thinking skills, their ability to raise issues competently and provide solutions under the chosen topic. The empirical nature of the research paper requires to collect data independently, present and analyze them properly and to use the results of the analysis in other decision making processes - not only discussing about new theories or models, but complementing them as well.

Report is an extended public written account on a certain topic in a particular study course.

Course paper is aimed to demonstrate the student’s abilities and research skills as well as his/her theoretical knowledge, according to the level of the course, in identifying and solving problems. The course paper will comply with the following requirements:

-it will be an independent research on a topical issue;

-it will contain accurate, clear and logical presentation of the progress of the research and its results, as well as conclusions and suggestions the author has arrived to;

-it will demonstrate the students grasp of using and evaluating specialized literature and other sources, gathering and processing information, as well as applying research methods and computer technologies.

Qualification paper, bachelor’s thesis and diploma paper are aimed to systematize, broaden and consolidate both the theoretical knowledge and the practical skills. The Paper will necessarily contain theoretical and practical parts. In the theoretical part the student will show his/her ability to research and solve a particular theoretical problem. The theoretical part will be in line with the practical part.
