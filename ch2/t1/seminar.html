Content of seminars for the course “Oil and gas factors in energy security provision”


Module 1: State factor on hydro energetic potential of Central Asia


Seminar 1. Questions of energy security provision in contemporary international relations

1. Concentration of production in a few, mainly unstable regions of the world.

2. The role of intense competition for energy resources among consumers.

3. Methods of more frequent and efficient use of energy as a foreign policy tool.


Seminar 2. Oil and gas energy security provision strategies

1. The interrelation of the concepts of oil and gas energy, security and foreign policy international relations.

2. The impact of developed energy relations with the regional/international powers and increased geostrategic significance on security of states with oil and gas ownership.

3. Foreign policies as a tool of energy security provision.


Seminar 3. Political and legal basis for energy security cooperation

1. The role of energy as a foreign policy issue in a state’s international relations.

2. International cooperation in the oil and gas sector as a tool for political development.


Seminar 4. The threat of terrorist domination in countries with oil and gas ownership

1. Syrian oil and the Islamic State of Iraq and Greater Syria.

2. International terrorist organizations with oil and gas provision possibilities.


Seminar 5. The impact of oil and gas policies of the world powers on international relations

1. The energy policy of the USA. Lifting of the Oil export ban.

2. The impact of oil and gas energy security on political and economic decisions of the European Union.

3. Role of Kazakhstan in the changing dynamics of international relations around Russian oil.
