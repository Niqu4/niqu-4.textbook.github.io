Task 1

One of the most important components of state power at all times has been the access to resources - land, water, energy and so on. However, along with scientific and technical progress, the rapid growth of industrial production, the transition to fossil fuels (coal and oil), and energy resources have taken a defining role in the development of states. The crucial role of oil, which has become the basis for a modern economy, most clearly manifested in the course of the Second World War, when the outcome of the fighting was largely dependent on a stable supply of crude oil and the possibility to produce fuel on a large scale.


Task 2

Today there is competition between the suppliers of energy resources, and it should also be built into the system of global energy security. The most important condition for this is to depoliticize energy security. In fact, the competition is built on the principles of economic competition for the consumer is a logical and understandable phenomenon. However, any use of energy resources for political purposes in the circumstances outstripping demand leads to conflicts, the development of which can be unpredictable. The very attempt to make energy into a necessary condition for economic growth, the subject of political confrontation is a challenge to global energy security system.


Task 3

In both the East China and South China seas, China and its neighbors claim assorted atolls and islands that sit astride vast undersea oil and gas reserves. The waters of both have been the site of recurring naval clashes over the past few years, with the South China Sea recently grabbing the spotlight.

An energy-rich offshoot of the western Pacific, that sea, long a focus of contention, is rimmed by China, Vietnam, the island of Borneo, and the Philippine Islands. When Vietnamese coast guard vessels attempted to penetrate this defensive ring in an effort to drive off the rig, they were rammed by Chinese ships and pummeled by water cannon. No lives have yet been lost in these encounters, but anti-Chinese rioting in Vietnam in response to the sea-borne encroachment left several dead and the clashes at sea are expected to continue for several months until the Chinese move the rig to another (possibly equally contested) location.

Task 4

The global aspect can approach to the energy resources not only as a means to trade and profit by individual players, but also as one of the foundations of economic and wider human development of the world as a whole. One of the outcomes of recent active discussions around the issue of energy security has become a growing awareness that energy, as well as, for example, the environment, has a global dimension - a sphere in which problems and challenges relate to the lives of every person in every country.


Task 5

The ultimate goal of the development of oil and gas energy markets is the formation of the global energy space with common rules of the “game”. And it will be easier to play for those how set the rules. So now those with power and influence will go fight for the long-term key positions in this space for many years before such a space will be finally formed.
