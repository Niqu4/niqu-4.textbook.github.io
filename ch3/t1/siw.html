Task 2 – 3hr

One of the threats to nuclear energy security provision is the vulnerability of nuclear plants.  Nuclear plant security measures are designed to protect three primary areas of vulnerability: controls on the nuclear chain reaction, cooling systems that prevent hot nuclear fuel from melting even after the chain reaction has stopped, and storage facilities for highly radioactive spent nuclear fuel. Most plants are designed and built to prevent dispersal of radioactivity, in the event of an accident, by surrounding the reactor in a steel-reinforced concrete containment structure. However, as the March 2011 Fukushima disaster in Japan demonstrated, reactor containments cannot completely block radioactive releases under the most severe circumstances, such as when cooling systems are disabled for several hours or longer.

This leads the world community to understand the need for a more efficient way of providing nuclear energy security.

After the 2011 Fukushima catastrophe Germany is known to have made crash tests of all its nuclear plants, and eventually closed down most of them. Turkey, on the other hand, continued building another nuclear plant in cooperation with Russia.

  1. Give an expert evaluation of the changes in international relations that can follow a major disastrous flaw in nuclear energy provision. What are the main factors that are likely to influence a shift in international relations or cooperation? Give a presentation of your report.

References: CRS Report for Congress Prepared for Members and Committees of Congress “Nuclear Power Plant Security and Vulnerabilities” Mark Holt, Anthony Andrews. January 3, 2014

Task 4 – 3hr

Ethanol from Brazilian sugarcane, which the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency classifies as an "advanced" biofuel, has made inroads into U.S. markets. According to a Wall Street Journal report, roughly 90 percent of advanced biofuels counted toward U.S. quotas in 2012 were imported, mostly from Brazil. Indeed, U.S. biofuel policy toward Latin America will remain a topic of debate in Washington, particularly related to Brazilian imports, given the competitive threat they pose to U.S. corn ethanol producers.

  1. Can this tendency be considered a step towards a change in foreign affairs of the USA?
  2. Write a report to highlight the changes in the external policy of a state of your choice connected with the growing need for alternative energy.

References: With renewable energy sources in countries like Brazil and Bolivia, will U.S. energy policy shift toward South America? Blake Clayton. Council on foreign relations: http://www.cfr.org/renewable-energy/renewable-energy-sources-countries-like-brazil-bolivia-us-energy-policy-shift-toward-south-america/p30268

 

Task 5 – 3hr

Announcing aspiration goals to elicit a windfall in foreign financing could prove a clever strategy to bootstrap a thriving renewables industry—but beyond the fanfare, few details are forthcoming on how the government will actually improve the investment climate and why renewable energy will meet India’s underlying energy needs.

  1. Make a prognosis for India’s possibilities in obtaining new connections for future international cooperation in political and economic spheres.
