CHAPTER DESCRIPTION

 

Chapter «Nuclear energy and the problems of alternative energy development» is one of the elective disciplines within the course “Energy Factor in International Relations” for speciality 5B020200 – “International Relations”.

Chapter objective – to create a common understanding of the problems of energy security provision in international relations; identify basic reasons for possible confrontations and international tension points in the field of nuclear energy security; identify methods for a comparative analysis in this area; create a knowledge of possible threats of nuclear energy to international relations and methods of counteractions; understanding of international threats connected with nuclear energy development; identifying reasons for international confrontations and disputes in the course of transition to alternative energy sources. Students develop a comprehensive picture of nuclear and alternative energy security policy and obtain an understanding of the possible methods of energy security provision.

During the course, students develop the skills to understand the various dimensions, which can be used to analyze international energy security.

Tasks of course: to generate skills on the basis of acquired knowledge, which are an integral part of model of the expert (Expert on the problems of the evolution of social and political factors of cooperation and perspectives for the implementation of sustainable development of energy in the framework of national interest’s security provision). The course should provide:

  • The knowledge of the basic principles of energy policy in general, different nuclear and alternative policies, nuclear energy security, alternative energy provision, the role of governments and companies, impact of policy instruments on the duration of international agreements.
  • The knowledge of the differences between political and economic diversification dimensions of energy security provision and the role of nuclear power and alternative resources.
  • The knowledge of energy problems of the world community, energy based relations on a global and regional scale, common mechanisms of foreign policies of different states in the nuclear energy sector.
  • The knowledge of perspectives of future development of alternative energy as a part of energy security provision strategies.
  • Skills of written comparative analysis.
  • Skills to determine the advisability of the research topic and comparative analysis regarding nuclear and alternative energy security.
  • Ability to work with sources and analyze them.
  • Ability to use of scientific and specialized terminology.
  • Ability to work in complex research, governmental and political environments.
  • Ability to use statistic and analytical materials.
  • Ability to understand the diversity of nuclear energy security concepts in various social and political situations of different states.
  • Ability to understand and analyze major nuclear energy security problems, taking into consideration specific internal and external policies of states and perspectives for transition to alternative energy.
  • The awareness of the technological advances in communication and the understanding of how these advances may affect the general reception of an expert opinion.

Content of lectures for the course “Nuclear energy and the problems of alternative energy development”

 

Module 1: Nuclear energy as a factor of energy security provision

 

Lecture 1. The role of nuclear energy in ensuring energy security of the states (with the example of Asia-Pacific states)

General overview of nuclear energy as a factor of energy security provision. Nuclear energy policies of the Asia-Pacific state. Perspectives of future development of nuclear energy security.

 

Lecture 2. The impact of technogenic factors on the nuclear policy of different states

Technogenic factors in international development of nuclear energy policies. Reactions of different states on technogenic threats to nuclear energy and security provision. Technogenic factors as a tool to change nuclear policies.

 

Lecture 3. The dynamics of international relations development in the framework of nuclear energy

Historical overview of changes in international relations in the light of nuclear energy development. Nuclear policies as a tool to political influence. International controversy in the nuclear sector.

 

Lecture 4. Main problems of nuclear energy security provision

High risks level. Possibilities for shifting from nuclear energy to nuclear weapons. Position of the Nuclear Club.

 

Lecture 5. The role of international organizations in the sphere of nuclear energy security provision

The role of UN in providing political cooperation in the sphere of nuclear energy security provision. The work of IAEA and NEA. Regional international organization as a tool for providing nuclear energy security.

 

Lecture 6. The nuclear policy of states as a factor of international conflicts escalation

Nuclear policies of states with developed nuclear energy. Threats to peaceful atom development. Fear of nuclear weapons development as a factor of escalating international tension.

Nuclear policy of the USA. The Iraq question. International disputes over non-proliferation and non-development. The North Korea threat.

 

Module 2: Future possibilities for alternative energy development

 

Lecture 7. The causes of the need for alternative energy resources

International confrontations in the nuclear energy sector as a major need for alternative energy. Technogenic threats of nuclear power. The need for renewable energy sources.

 

Lecture 8. The impact of alternative energy resources on the foreign policy of states

Alternative energy resources as a tool for international cooperation and possible integration. Transition to alternative energy as a result of international political pressure.

 

Lecture 9. Principles of the change in energy policy in connection with the transition to alternative energy resources

Examples of changes in energy policies of states. Main aspects of reversion from nuclear energy provision. The case of the Republic of Kazakhstan.

 

Lecture 10. International legal mechanisms of regulation of interstate relations in the field of alternative energy (IRENA, UN, ITER)

Main international legal mechanisms of regulating alternative energy development. Role of international organization in promoting alternative energy development as a method of energy security provision. The work of IRENA and ITER in the field of alternative energy development and international cooperation in this sector.

 

Lecture 11. Alternative energy as a way of global integration

Political background for international integration on the basis of alternative energy security provision. Regional hydro energy cooperation. International solar energy projects. Changes in foreign policies of states as a result of alternative energy development.

 

Lecture 12. The perspectives of alternate energy

Possibilities for further international cooperation and development of alternative energy as a way of international energy security provision. Perspectives for alternative energy security provision in states with lack of fossil fuels, oil and gas.

 

Lecture 13. Cooperation of the states of Central Asia in the sphere of alternate energy

Perspectives of interstate cooperation in Central Asia in the sphere of alternative energy. Hydro power disputes between Central Asian states. Possibilities of international cooperation in the region with the help of foreign energy security policies.

 

Lecture 14. The role of the Republic of Kazakhstan in ensuring energy security in view of the transition to alternative energy resources

Historical overview of the nuclear policy of Kazakhstan. Kazakhstan’s example as a demonstration of successful energy security provision policy. The types of alternative energy sources developed in the state.

 

Content of seminars for the course “Nuclear energy and the problems of alternative energy development”

 

Module 1: Nuclear energy as a factor of energy security provision

 

Seminar 1. Nuclear power in international energy security provision

  1. The dynamics of the development of the nuclear part of energy security.
  2. Pursue of nuclear power generation by Northeast Asian countries to enhance energy security in the face of dwindling regional oil supplies.

 

Seminar 2. The impact of technogenic factors in nuclear energy on nuclear policies of states

  1. Technogenic threats to nuclear energy security.
  2. The lessons of Chernobyl.
  3. Fukushima Daiichi disaster and the changes in energy policies of the world community.

 

Seminar 3. The development of international relations in the framework of nuclear energy security provision

  2. Foreign policies of developed nuclear states towards countries with developing nuclear power.
  3. Nuclear development limitations.

 

Seminar 4. International organizations in the sphere of nuclear energy security provision

  1. UN and the IAEA in the framework of nuclear energy security provision.
  2. The role of regional organizations in providing energy security in the nuclear sector.

 

Seminar 5. Nuclear power generation and energy security: the challenges and possibilities of regional cooperation

  1. Growing public doubts over the necessity of nuclear energy development in different regions.
  2. Regional nuclear cooperation with the example of Russia.

 

Module 2: Future possibilities for alternative energy development

 

Seminar 6. The causes of the need for alternative energy resources

  1. Historical background of alternative energy development.
  2. Contemporary possibilities of alternative energy security provision.

 

Seminar 7. Principles of the change in energy policy in connection with the transition to alternative energy resources

  1. Major renewable energy policies and barriers.
  2. International clean energy supply programs.
  3. Perspectives for global alternative energy development partnerships.

 

Seminar 8. International organizations for regulation of interstate relations in the field of alternative energy

  1. The IEA in promoting renewable energy security provision.

The methodical recommendations for studying the discipline “Nuclear energy and the problems of alternative energy development”

The purpose of these methodological guidelines is to present the basic requirements of Research Paper (hereinafter – RP) for students studying the discipline “Nuclear energy and the problems of alternative energy development”.

Students have to deliver written work of different complexity:

Essay. This is the smallest scale, well-structured, consistent, easy-to-read written work, where the student can clearly express his/her approach, analysis and solutions to different claims and problems presented in scientific literature.

Descriptive or synoptic paper. This pattern of work is greater in volume than the essay, it presents scientifically valid opinions and their critical evaluation in a consistent way and the assessment of this type of written work usually makes up a solid part of accumulative mark. 

Research paper. It is written in frequent consultation with supervisors. Its goal is to deepen the knowledge acquired during lectures and practical seminars, to develop students’ critical thinking skills, their ability to raise issues competently and provide solutions under the chosen topic. The empirical nature of the research paper requires to collect data independently, present and analyze them properly and to use the results of the analysis in other decision making processes - not only discussing about new theories or models, but complementing them as well.

Report is an extended public written account on a certain topic in a particular study course.

Course paper is aimed to demonstrate the student’s abilities and research skills as well as his/her theoretical knowledge, according to the level of the course, in identifying and solving problems. The course paper will comply with the following requirements:

-it will be an independent research on a topical issue;

-it will contain accurate, clear and logical presentation of the progress of the research and its results, as well as conclusions and suggestions the author has arrived to;

-it will demonstrate the students grasp of using and evaluating specialized literature and other sources, gathering and processing information, as well as applying research methods and computer technologies.

Qualification paper, bachelor’s thesis and diploma paper are aimed to systematize, broaden and consolidate both the theoretical knowledge and the practical skills. The Paper will necessarily contain theoretical and practical parts. In the theoretical part the student will show his/her ability to research and solve a particular theoretical problem. The theoretical part will be in line with the practical part.
